About

Born and raised in Cairo, I developed an interest in building and breaking things or, in other words, software engineering and even more so in information security during my teenage years. Misled by Hollywood's depiction of a hooded-hacker sitting in their basement going about their nightly business, I started using some crude tools such as Sub7, Visual Basic, and some social engineering techniques to hack into my friends' computers. Despite managing to social engineer my way into getting some passwords and sensitive information, I probably infected my computer with a heap of malware rather than successfully broke into my friends' devices!

Majoring in computer science at the American University in Cairo (AUC), I enrolled in every single security- and network-focused course I could enrol in to gather as much academic information about the security domain as possible. In 2008, that was nowhere near enough to land a job at the few Egyptian companies that worked within the security domain. Funny how far the industry came and how many security-focused job opportunities one can find nowadays. Well, I thought maybe another degree in information security can help me find a job. So, I went for a master's degree in information security at University College London (UCL) between 2011 and 2012.

During my master's at UCL, I decided upon further pursuing the academic research aspect of information security by going for a doctoral degree. Unfortunately, I could not secure funding for any of the doctoral programs that accepted me in the UK. So, I moved back to Cairo to work as an information security researcher for a mere of one year before finally moving to Munich, Germany for a second master's degree at the TU Munich, and a doctoral degree I am currently working on.

My primary research focus is on utilizing machine learning techniques and algorithms within the domains of malware analysis/detection focusing on Android malware. After five years of grinding and getting a good number of my papers rejected, I successfully defended my doctoral thesis entitled "Accurate and Reliable Labeling for Effective Detection of Android Malware" and decided that I would never pursue a career in academia (i.e., enough is enough).

Ever since I have been helping firms build and maintain their security programs. My tasks range from the tiresome creation and enforcement of ISO27*-/SOC2-compliant security policies to writing (secure) code and everything in the middle: penetration testing, writing tools to scan apps and code bases for potential vulnerabilities, hosting security awareness talks, threat modeling, etc.

Additionally, since late 2021, I have been part of a hobby project that morphed into a GmbH (German for LLC) that we named StayrAI. As a co-founder, my tasks basically span everything. But I really enjoy the part about developing our apps in Flutter, Dart, and Go, setting up and maintaining our AWS-based cloud infrastructure, and anything that has to do with security.

And that's all folks! That's the story of my life. :D
