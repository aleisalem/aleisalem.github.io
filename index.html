About

bash — aleisalem@gridx: ~
$ whoami --verbose

Born and raised in Cairo, I developed an interest in building and breaking things — software engineering and information security — during my teenage years. Misled by Hollywood's depiction of a hooded hacker, I started with crude tools like Sub7, Visual Basic, and social engineering to "hack" friends' computers. I probably infected my own machine more than theirs.

Majoring in Computer Science at the American University in Cairo, I enrolled in every security- and network-focused course I could find. A Master's in Information Security at University College London followed, then a second Master's and a doctoral degree at TU Munich — defended in 2021 on Android malware detection using machine learning.

Since then, I have been helping firms build and scale their security programs: ISO 27001 and SOC 2 ISMS implementations, writing detection rules, coordinating pen tests, building CI/CD scanning pipelines, and running security awareness campaigns. The blend of researcher, engineer, and pragmatic risk manager.

Currently serving as Head of Security at gridX GmbH, an E.ON subsidiary building digital energy infrastructure — where the stakes of getting security right are very real.
